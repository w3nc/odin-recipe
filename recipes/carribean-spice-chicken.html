Carribean Spice Chicken

Los Pollos Hermanos's Carribean Spice Chicken

Channel your inner Gus Fring with this recipe inspired by Breaking Bad and Better Call Saul. The secret to Los Pollos Hermanos-style fried chicken is a robust Caribbean spice blend, a buttermilk-pickle marinade, and a double-dredging technique for maximum crispiness .

Ingredients

The Secret Spice Blend

  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 2 tbsp ground cumin
  • 2 tbsp onion powder
  • 2 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt

Marinade

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into 10 classic pieces
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • ¼ cup pickle juice

Dredge & Batter

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 2 eggs
  • Vegetable or peanut oil for frying

Steps

  1. Season: Combine all the spices to make your secret blend. Sprinkle about 2 tablespoons of this over your chicken pieces and rub it in.
  2. Marinate: Mix the buttermilk, pickle juice, and 2 tablespoons of the spice blend. Submerge the chicken, cover, and refrigerate for 4 to 24 hours.
  3. Prep the Breading: Whisk the flour and cornstarch in one bowl, and the eggs in another.
  4. Double-Dredge: Take the chicken out of the marinade. Dip each piece into the egg wash, coat it thoroughly in the flour mixture, dip it back into the egg wash, and coat it in the flour one more time. Let the chicken rest for 10 minutes so the batter adheres.
  5. Fry: Heat 2 inches of oil in a heavy-bottomed pot to 350°F (175°C). Fry the chicken in batches for 7 to 10 minutes, or until the skin is a deep golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
