Signature Spice Curls

Los Pollos Hermanos's Signature Spice Curls

Los Pollos Hermanos Signature Spice Curls are a premium, southwest-style curly fry featuring a distinctive zesty kick and a hyper-crispy exterior. Introduced in Better Call Saul by Madrigal Electromotive’s fast-food division, they are crafted from spiralized russet potatoes coated in a seasoned flour batter. The proprietary spice blend leans heavily on regional flavors, balancing the heat of cayenne and Aleppo peppers with the warmth of smoked paprika and coriander.

Ingredients

For the Southwest Spice Blend

  • 15 g Cayenne pepper
  • 10 g Aleppo pepper (or ancho chili powder)
  • 10 g Ground coriander
  • 15 g Red pepper flakes (finely ground)
  • 15 g Smoked paprika
  • 6 g Onion powder
  • 1 tbsp Garlic powder
  • To taste Kosher salt and black pepper

For the Spud Base & Butter

  • 2 large Russet Potatoes
  • 60 g All-purpose flour
  • 150 g Water
  • 1 quart Peanut oil (or vegetable oil for frying)

Steps

  1. Spiralize and Prep the Potatoes
    • Prep the curls: Process the scrubbed russet potatoes through a spiralizer using a curly-fry blade attachment.
    • Cold soak: Submerge the raw curls into a large bowl of ice-cold water.
    • Rest: Leave them for 15 minutes to strip away excess starch, ensuring maximum crunch.
    • Dry thoroughly: Drain the water and press the curls between heavy layers of paper towels until completely bone-dry.
  2. Construct the Seasoned Batter
    • Combine spices: Whisk the cayenne, Aleppo pepper, coriander, red pepper flakes, paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder evenly in a small dish.
    • Mix the wet dredge: In a separate mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, 3 tablespoons of your custom southwest spice blend, and 150 grams of water until a smooth, thin batter forms.
  3. Coat and Fry
    • Heat the oil: Pour your frying oil into a deep cast-iron skillet or heavy pot and bring the temperature up to exactly 350°F (175°C).
    • Dunk: Toss the dried potato curls into the seasoned batter, shaking away any excessive dripping coating.
    • Fry in batches: Carefully drop the battered curls into the hot oil. Avoid overcrowding the pot so the oil temperature does not crash.
    • Cook until golden: Fry for 4 to 6 minutes, turning occasionally, until they turn deep golden brown and hyper-crispy.
    • Drain and serve: Fish the spice curls out with tongs, let them drain on a wire cooling rack, and immediately dust them with a final pinch of kosher salt and leftover dry spice mix while hot.
